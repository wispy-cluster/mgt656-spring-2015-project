Application Progress Update:

 

Further to the story status table above, the application is working and we are working on improving the layout and appearance of the website. These will be completed during the next two days.  We successfully incorporated features related to creating a search function and sorting the event dates in a descending order which were requirements for teams with 5 members.  

 

Estimate the story points

 

Similar to previous sprints, each team member was asked to privately rank the difficulty of stories on a scale from 1-4.  We then compared everyone’s list to see which stories were considered the most difficult and then allocated stories so that points would be distributed according to team members ability.  

 

Overall Review of Sprint 6

 

We were able to complete 8 stories out of 11 originally planned for Sprint 5, or 72% of the stories. We found some of the stories (e.g. re-directing to event details upon submission of event) challenging and had to meet a couple of times to brainstorm the way forward. The create event addition code was changed to include a function ‘isRangeInt’ to validate the forms, and to include the drop-down functionality for certain event attributes such as month and day. The API was updated to so as to allow search for events using any detail keyword as well as email ID of attending users. While we managed to overcome the hurdles related to the aforementioned stories, we weren’t able to get to stories around project analytics.

 

Future Plans

For our next sprint we plan to complete the 3 remaining stories.  In addition, we plan to invest susbstantial time in improving the layout and format of various pages and have already allocated the work amongst team members. The plan is to meet daily over the next couple of days and complete the project by 9th Dec. We will keep the last 2 days as buffer for any last minute requirements/changes.
