Application Progress Update:

 

Further to the story status table above, the application is working except for creating new event page and the new stories that were added by the instructor.  While the event page itself has been completed, the associated stories tests do not pass because we don’t have a submit button or the drop down lists.  This will be completed during Sprint 5.  We also received two new requirements which we will need to complete during the final sprint.  One is related to creating a function that allow attendeeds to make donations to the event.  The second is related to the fact we are group of 5 so we will also need to create a search function and an analytics function.  Given we only have one week to complete this, it will be a relatively intense week and hence we have decided to meet several times before our final submission.  If the above is completed prior to due date we hope to also add formatting changes to improve the site’s lay-out and apperance.  

 

Lastly, you will also notice the burn chart slope has decreased as a result of the additional stories that were added.  We have not included Story 6 in the burndown chart because we do not plan to have any stories to complete by this time as we will only have 2 days to do so.  Instead we will use the time to reflect on lessons learned and how the process could have been improved for future sprints/projects.

 

Estimate the story points

 

Similar to previous sprints, each team member was asked to privately rank the difficulty of stories on a scale from 1-4.  We then compared everyone’s list to see which stories were considered the most difficult and then allocated stories so that points would be distributed according to team members ability.  

 

Overall Review of Sprint 5

 

We were able to complete 10 stories out of 11 originally planned for Sprint 4, or 89% of the stories. As we were not able to meet due to the holiday, we spent the majority of the meeting reviewing the new stories that were added to the backlog. We also changed the order of several stories to follow the work sequence of the page.  For example, we put create event page button ahead of event page to correspond with site usage order of operations.  One question we had was how would we increase traffice to 50-100 users a day?  If it is a seperate program that does this, how will this help us understand our user interface if an algorithm ultimately dictates how the “traffic” interacts with our page.  We also hope to get a better sense of how these requirements can be broken down into discrete stories so that we can divide the work more efficiently. One of the team member is planning on going to office hours tomorrow.

 

Future Plans

For our next sprint we plan to complete the 12 remaining stories.  However, we believe this will turn out to be more than 12 stories because the new requirements likely comprise multiple stories.  We also have planned to meet two more times to not only keep pace with the substantial workload ahead but also review any issues we have and leverage our collective knowledge to help one another.
