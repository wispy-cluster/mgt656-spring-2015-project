Estimate the story points

 

We were able to complete 9 out of 10 stories for Sprint-2 giving us more confidence in our ability to not only estimate story points and durations but also our understanding of the webstack is improving.  Similar to the previous two sprints, in order to estimate the length of our stories we used a combination of a relative size vs. time estimate and planning poker (without the cards) to approximate story completion times as this seemed to be effective at gaging the difficulty of each story.

 

Similar to Sprint-2, each team member was asked to privately rank stories they felt the team would be able to complete in the coming week.  We then compared everyone’s list to see which stories were considered doable by all members and which were not.  Every story that was considered doable for the week was added to our sprint list and the rest were discussed to understand why group members believed they were not suited for this week’s spirit.  Through this conversation we realized that these tasks were achievable (we realized some team members had a better grasp of the story requirements) and so were added to Sprint-3 story log.  

 

For stories that were not included on anyone’s list, we decided we were going to check progress during our next sprint meeting.  

 

Overall Review of Sprint 3

 

After reviewing the previous week’s work, the group agreed that we were generally happier with our progress to date as we complete 80% of the stories we originally set out to complete during the past week.  We have about 32 stories remaining and we predict more stories will be added as we refine our webpage and receive additional stories from the instructor.  We estimated about 20 new stories bringing total remaining stories to roughly 50.  Given we have 4 weeks remaining to complete the project, we have to strive to complete roughly 13 stories/week.

 

While it is unadvisable to conduct sprint review meetings during actual sprint meetings we had no choice but to consolidate meetings due to everyone’s busy schedule.  During our review we agreed that the current workflow of merging stories ourselves (only after they have been tested) was a good strategy and was efficient.  We also agreed that if any story is added later on, such as the visual layout of the webpage, we would wait to review as a group before merging.

 

We then went through the remaining user stories and distributed tasks based on current comfort level with coding of individual team members. While the work ahead this week is ambitious we decided to add several stories to make up for the slower productivity of week 1.  


 

Future Plans

For our next sprint we plan to complete approximately 15 stories.  We believe this is achievable given that many of the stories are similar (i.e. Story 28 should display errors to the user when the month is not an integer) in that we need to display an error when a user fails to fill out the form with all the required information. We are planning to hold another scrum meeting at the end of this week to analyze our progress and performance. During this meeting, we also plan to discuss the remaining backlog, tests, and what should be accepted as a complete story.
