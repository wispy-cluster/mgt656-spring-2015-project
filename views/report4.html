Estimate the story points

 

Similar to previous sprints, each team member was asked to privately rank stories they felt the team would be able to complete in the coming week.  We then compared everyone’s list to see which stories were considered doable by all members and which were not.  Every story that was considered doable for the week was added to our sprint list and the rest were discussed to understand why group members believed they were not suited for this week’s spirit.   

 

Overall Review of Sprint 4

 

We were able to complete 12 stories out of 15 stories originally planned for Sprint 4, which is about 80% of the stories we set out to complete. However, we ended up completing 4 additional stories related to error messages for new event creating. Hence, we completed 16 stories in total during Sprint 4. Stories that were not completed are related to the “Event Details page.' Brian was responsible for those stories, and he was able to figure out how to display the event’s details page on the app, but the test program did not pass the tests related to that function. He was not able to find out why the test program does not work with the specific function and could not proceed with the other two stories since they are related. We planned to get some consultation with the issue and figure out how to solve it in Sprint 5. The completed stories are related to new event creation page and the error messages related to creating new events. The stories were marked complete because they passed the test program, but we were not able to test them on the app ourselves since we do not have a submit function with the ‘new event creation page’ yet. We plan to add submit button and verify the stories ourselves in story 5.

 

Future Plans

For our next sprint we plan to complete all the current remaining 18 stories.  We believe this is achievable based on the velocity shown by the team during previous sprints. Over successive sprints, team members have become more comfortable with coding, committing and managing the process overall. In the remaining sprints, we plan to improve formatting, do further tests by actualling using the app and tackle any new stories that might be added later.
